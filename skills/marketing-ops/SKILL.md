## The 3 LinkedIn Bottlenecks

Every business owner on LinkedIn has one of three core problems blocking revenue:

### Bottleneck 1: Confusing Messaging
Positioning is unclear. Visitors don't immediately understand what you do, who you help, or why you're different.
**Symptoms:** High views, low saves. People scroll past without engaging.
**Fix:** Apply the Positioning Pyramid — Profile → Offer → Audience → Content — before creating any content.

### Bottleneck 2: Cringy Sales
You know what you sell but cannot talk about it without feeling salesy. You avoid promoting or soft-pedal your offers.
**Symptoms:** Good engagement, zero leads. People love your content but don't become clients.
**Fix:** Frame your offer as a transformation with specific numbers — not a service description.

### Bottleneck 3: Overlooked Marketing
Positioning is clear and offer is good, but nobody sees your content. You're posting into a void.
**Symptoms:** Inconsistent posting, no momentum, followers but no traction.
**Fix:** Build a Content Flywheel with ICP-targeted content and consistency streaks. Post 4x/week minimum.

## Authority Signals Framework

Five signals establish authority on LinkedIn. All five must be present for high-ticket closes:

| Signal | What It Means | Example |
|--------|--------------|---------|
| **Results** | Specific numbers, named outcomes | "$2.1M in revenue for clients in 90 days" |
| **Expertise** | Frameworks, systems, processes you built | "I created the Message Architecture Canvas used by 200+ founders" |
| **Social Proof** | Who trusted you — named clients, recognizable companies | "Used by teams at Stripe, Notion, and Linear" |
| **Recognition** | Credentials, awards, features | "Forbes 30 Under 30", "LinkedIn Top Creator 3 years" |
| **Proof** | Before/after transformations, testimonials, case studies | Named client, specific numbers, real timeline |
