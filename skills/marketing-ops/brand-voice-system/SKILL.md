# Brand Voice & Tone System

For clients ranging from Kinderspeech to Harley-Davidson, a uniform "human-like" writing style is not enough. This system defines **measurable brand voice fingerprints** that differentiate every piece of copy.

## Architecture

```
BRAND IDENTITY (fixed) — who the brand is
    ↓
VOICE AXES (numeric sliders) — measurable dimensions
    ↓
TONE MODULATION (context matrix) — situation deltas
    ↓
COPYWRITER PROMPT → body copy
    ↓
VOICE SCORECARD (enforcement gate) — verify adherence
```

## Layer 1: Brand Identity

### Archetype Model (Jung / Mark-Pearson)

12 archetypes grouped by motivation. Every brand gets one primary + one secondary.

|------------|-----------|-------------|---------------|----------|
| **Structure** | Ruler | Control, order | Rolex, Microsoft, Mercedes | authoritative, premium, established |
| **Structure** | Creator | Innovation, self-expression | Apple, Lego, Adobe | visionary, craft, original |
| **Structure** | Caregiver | Help, protect | Dove, Johnson & Johnson, Patagonia | nurturing, warm, supportive |
| **Connect** | Jester | Fun, lighten up | Old Spice, M&M's, Skittles | playful, irreverent, surprising |
| **Connect** | Everyman | Belong, connect | IKEA, Target, Levi's | relatable, down-to-earth, honest |
| **Connect** | Lover | Intimacy, passion | Chanel, Victoria's Secret, Godiva | sensual, indulgent, desire |

### Aaker's Big Five Scores (1–7)

Score the brand on each dimension. These are measurable and enforceable.

| Dimension | What it measures | Low (1–2) | Mid (3–5) | High (6–7) |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------|-----------|------------|
| **Sincerity** | Down-to-earth, honest, wholesome, cheerful | Cynical, detached | Balanced | Warm, earnest, family-friendly |
| **Excitement** | Daring, spirited, imaginative, up-to-date | Staid, conservative | Moderate energy | Buzzworthy, trendy, bold |
| **Competence** | Reliable, intelligent, successful | Casual, loose | Dependable | Authoritative, expert, premium |
| **Sophistication** | Upper-class, charming, glamorous | Rough, utilitarian | Balanced | Elegant, luxurious, refined |
| **Ruggedness** | Outdoorsy, tough, masculine | Delicate, polished | Neutral | Rugged, durable, tough |

Examples:
- **Harley-Davidson**: Sincerity 2, Excitement 6, Competence 3, Sophistication 1, Ruggedness 7
- **Disney (The Innocent's innocence with Magician's wonder)**: Sincerity 7, Excitement 5, Competence 4, Sophistication 3, Ruggedness 1
- **Mailchimp**: Sincerity 5, Excitement 4, Competence 5, Sophistication 3, Ruggedness 1
- **Dove (Caregiver)**: Sincerity 7, Excitement 2, Competence 5, Sophistication 2, Ruggedness 1

### 3–5 Voice Characteristics (X but not Y)

Agency-standard: each characteristic is a **tension**, not a flat statement. This prevents over-rotation.

For **Harley-Davidson** (Outlaw/Explorer):
- Rebellious but not reckless
- Authentic but not crude
- Confident but not arrogant
- Independent but not lonely
- American but not jingoistic

For **Kinderspeech** (Caregiver/Innocent):
- Warm but not saccharine
- Simple but not dumbed-down
- Reassuring but not alarmist
- Expert but not clinical
- Gentle but not vague

For **Mailchimp** (Everyman/Jester):
- Human but not chummy
- Familiar but not corporate
- Friendly but not pushy
- Knowledgeable but not a know-it-all

For **Apple** (Creator/Magician):
- Minimal but not cold
- Confident but not boastful
- Visionary but not abstract
- Simple but not simplistic

For **Tesla** (Magician/Hero):
- Bold but not reckless
- Visionary but not ungrounded
- Disruptive but not destructive
- Intelligent but not elitist

### Signature Moves (2–4) + Banned Moves (2–4)

**Signature moves** = rhetorical devices the brand owns. Part of its verbal identity fingerprint.

| Brand | Signature Moves |
|-------|----------------|
| Apple | Short declaratives, noun-as-verb ("Think different"), en dash for emphasis, article-dropping headlines |
| Innocent Drinks | Parenthetical asides, lowercase brand name, conversation fragments |
| Oatly | Self-aware meta-copy, varied sentence rhythm, conversational footnotes |
| Mailchimp | Dry humor in error states, unexpected metaphors, "It's [adjective]." sentence fragments |
| Harley-Davidson | Imperatives ("Live by your own rules"), fragment emphatics ("No apologies."), sensory texture words |
| Kinderspeech | Gentle questions ("What does your child need?"), inclusive "we" ("how we help"), simple declaratives |
| Nike | Imperatives ("Just do it"), short parallel clauses, second-person direct address |
| Monzo | Direct address, question hooks, word-swap tables (purchase→buy, require→need) |

**Banned moves** = devices the brand never uses.

| Brand | Banned |
|-------|--------|
| Apple | Exclamation marks in alerts, anthropomorphizing device, blaming user, jargon without explanation |
| Harley-Davidson | Corporate speak, safety-first language, hedging, bureaucratic phrases |
| Kinderspeech | Alarmist language, clinical "patient" instead of "child," jargon, condescending tone |
| Mailchimp | Inside jokes, excessive corporate-speak, humor at user's expense, superlatives |

## Layer 2: Voice Axes (Numeric Sliders)

Expanded from Nielsen Norman Group's 4 dimensions + industry extensions. **These determine writing behavior.**

| Axis | Min (0) | Mid (5) | Max (10) | What it changes |
|------|---------|---------|----------|-----------------|
| **Formality** | Slang, fragments, contractions | Neutral, "you" address | No contractions, full sentences, Latinate vocab | Contraction use, sentence formality |
| **Humor** | Bone-dry, no jokes, no wordplay | Occasional wry aside | Jokes, puns, surprise, irony | Joke density, playful language |
| **Warmth** | Cold, distant, transactional | Neutral, professional | Warm, personal, "we're in this together" | Personal address, empathy markers |
| **Enthusiasm** | Flat, understated, matter-of-fact | Moderate energy | Excited, exclamation-rich, high energy | Exclamation marks, intensity adverbs |
| **Confidence** | Hedged ("we think", "possibly") | Balanced | Declarative ("we know", "this is") | Hedge markers, declarative sentences |
| **Concreteness** | Abstract concepts, nominalizations | Mixed | Sensory specifics, examples, numbers | Ratio of concrete to abstract language |
| **Directness** | Suggestions, questions, invitations | Mixed | Imperatives, commands, short declaratives | Command frequency, question ratio |
| **Sentence Rhythm** | Uniform (similar lengths) | Mixed | High variance (fragments to long) | Sentence length distribution |

### Reading Level Target

- **Under 5th grade**: DTC consumer, kids products, social media
- **5th–7th grade**: General consumer email, e-commerce, apps (Mailchimp, Apple)
- **8th–9th grade**: B2B SaaS, financial challengers (Monzo G9)
- **10th–12th grade**: Professional services, legal, enterprise
- **College+**: Academic, luxury, high-end thought leadership

## Layer 3: Tone Modulation (Context Matrix)

Voice stays fixed. Tone modulates based on context.

### Modulation Axes

Apply deltas to the baseline sliders based on:

| Context | Delta Applied | Example |
|---------|--------------|---------|
| **Error / failure state** | Warmth +2, Directness -2 | "Something went wrong" → warmer, less imperative |
| **Critical info / legal** | Formality +3, Humor -3 | Terms of service → drop all humor |
| **Onboarding / welcome** | Warmth +2, Enthusiasm +1 | First email → warmer, more guiding |
| **Sales / promotional** | Enthusiasm +2, Directness +1 | Campaign email → more energy |
| **Support / help** | Warmth +3, Confidence -1 | Troubleshooting → gentle, empathetic |
| **Crisis / urgent** | Formality +1, Confidence +2, Humor -5 | Emergency → direct, no humor |
| **Social media** | Formality -2, Humor +1, Rhythm +1 | Instagram → looser, punchier |
| **Internal / docs** | Formality +0, Warmth -1, Humor -2 | API docs → clear, neutral |

### Mailchimp Model (Best Example)

Mailchimp's tone matrix maps **user emotional state** to tone:

| If user is... | Tone becomes... |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Delighted | Match energy. Celebrate with them. |
| Curious / exploring | Clear, helpful. Answer questions before they ask. |
| Confused | Drop humor. Simple sentences. Direct. |
| Frustrated | Empathetic first. Then solution. No blame. |
| Anxious | Reassuring. Human. Simple. Never dismissive. |
| Successful / completed | Congratulate briefly. Then next steps. |

## Layer 5: Voice Enforcement (The Gate)

After copy is written with a brand profile, verify it against the profile before delivery.

### Voice Scorecard (Checker Subagent Prompt)

When checking copy against a brand profile, the voice checker subagent scores the copy on every voice axis. For each axis, it assigns a score (0–10) and states whether it matches the target within ±1.5. If any axis is off by more than 1.5, the copy needs revision.

Example output:
```
formality: target 3, actual 6 → OFF by 3 (too formal, use contractions and shorter sentences)
warmth: target 6, actual 7 → OK
humor: target 5, actual 2 → OFF by 3 (too dry, add playfulness)
enthusiasm: target 5, actual 5 → OK
confidence: target 5, actual 7 → OFF by 2 (too declarative, soften claims)
```

**Hard enforcement for banned items:**
- If a banned move is detected → FAIL. The copy must be rewritten.
- If a signature move is absent across an entire piece → WARN. Add at least one.
- If the reading level is more than 2 grades off → FAIL. Rewrite with shorter/longer sentences.

### Voice Profile — Embedding into Copywriter Prompts

Before writing copy for any brand, inject the full profile into the Copywriter's context:

```
BRAND VOICE PROFILE:
Archetype: Outlaw/Explorer (primary/secondary)
Aaker Dimension Scores: Sincerity 2, Excitement 6, Competence 3, Sophistication 1, Ruggedness 7

VOICE AXES:
- Formality: 1/10 (fragments OK, contractions always, no corporate speak)
- Humor: 5/10 (wry, dry — not jokey)
- Warmth: 3/10 (direct, not cold — but not warm either)
- Enthusiasm: 7/10 (energetic but not hyped)
- Confidence: 8/10 (declarative, no hedging)
- Concreteness: 8/10 (sensory, tactile, specific)
- Directness: 8/10 (imperatives, commands, no beating around)
- Sentence Rhythm: 8/10 (high variance: fragments to long flow)

CHARACTERISTICS (X but not Y):
1. Rebellious but not reckless
2. Authentic but not crude
3. Confident but not arrogant
4. Independent but not lonely

SIGNATURE MOVES (use at least 2):
- Imperatives and commands
- Short emphatic fragments
- Sensory texture words (thunder, asphalt, chrome, leather)
- Declarative brand statements

BANNED MOVES (never):
- Corporate speak or MBA language
- Hedging or uncertainty
- Precious or delicate language
- Over-explaining
```

## Adding a New Brand Profile

To add a new brand voice to the system:

1. **Identify the primary and secondary archetypes** — use the 12 archetype table above
2. **Score Aaker's 5 dimensions 1–7** — based on the brand's actual messaging
3. **Set the 8 voice axes 0–10** — baseline from the archetype defaults in the table, adjust for the specific brand
4. **Write 3–5 "X but not Y" characteristics** — these define the boundaries
5. **List 2–4 signature moves + 2–4 banned moves** — concrete rhetorical devices
6. **Set reading level target** — based on audience
7. **Write 3 example sentences** in the brand voice — canonical examples for the Copywriter to match

Store new profiles in `references/brand-profiles/` as YAML files.

---

## Putting It Together: End-to-End Workflow

1. **Identify the brand voice profile** — pick from templates above or build new
2. **Inject the profile into the Copywriter prompt** — using the full voice profile format
3. **Copywriter produces draft** — writing against all voice dimensions
4. **humanize-verify.py** — run the general AI-writing-quality gate (catches Tier 1 words, em dashes, etc.)
5. **Voice scorecard check** — delegate to a subagent that scores the copy against the brand profile
6. **Iterate** — if any axis is off by >1.5 or any banned move is found, send back with delta
7. **Deliver** — copy that passes both gates

## Quick-Reference Voice Differences

| Aspect | Kinderspeech (Caregiver) | Harley-Davidson (Outlaw) | Apple (Creator) | Mailchimp (Everyman) |
|--------|------------------------|------------------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Sentence length | Short-med (10–18 words) | Short (8–14 words) | Short (10–16 words) | Varied (6–25 words) |
| Contractions | Yes, always | Yes, always | Sometimes | Yes, always |
| Exclamation marks | Rare | Occasional | Never in UI | Dry, occasional |
| Second person | Optional "you" | Direct "you" | Direct "you" | Direct "you" |
| Humor | Gentle, never at user expense | Dry, wry | Almost none | Dry, unexpected |
| Emotional register | Warm, reassuring | Bold, intense | Confident, cool | Friendly, human |
| Jargon level | Minimal, explained | Minimal | Controlled | Minimal |
| Reading grade | 5th | 6th | 7th | 7th |
| Paragraph length | 3–4 sentences | 1–3 sentences | 2–3 sentences | 2–4 sentences |
