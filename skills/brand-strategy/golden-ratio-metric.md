## About This Skill

The Golden Ratio Metric is the 3:1 LTV:CAC ratio — the single metric that determines whether a business model is sustainable or merely temporarily propped up by outside capital. All other metrics are vanity; this one is economic reality.

## Key Concepts

- **LTV:CAC 3:1 Minimum**: Above this ratio = efficient growth engine; below = cash-burning machine that will collapse when capital runs out
- **True LTV Calculation**: Total revenue over entire customer relationship, including renewals, upsells, and expansions — not just first transaction
- **True CAC Calculation**: Total cost to acquire one customer including content, ads, tools, time — not just ad spend on the credit card statement
- **Death of Arbitrage**: The 2015 DTC playbook (Facebook Ads + Shopify + VC) no longer works in 2026 due to privacy-first framework elimination of reliable attribution

## How to Use This Skill

Use when a client is focused on vanity metrics or considering aggressive paid acquisition. Force the LTV:CAC calculation first. If they can't achieve 3:1 with current unit economics, the paid acquisition strategy needs revision before scaling. The Brandformance approach builds LTV first, then acquires customers efficiently, not the other way around.
