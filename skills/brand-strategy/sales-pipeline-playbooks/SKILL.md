# Business & Sales Pipeline Playbooks

## Structure, not personality

These patterns are extracted structural engines. Do not quote the original source. Do not reference the teacher. Fill the slots with the current business's variables and deploy.

## 1. LAPS Pipeline (Pipeline Engine)

A weekly funnel that converts attention into money. The business runs on the rhythm, not on spikes.

```text
Leads → Appointments → Presentations → Sales
```

### Slots to fill per business:
- **Lead target per week** (e.g. 50)
- **Appointment rate** (e.g. 20% of leads → 10)
- **Presentation rate** (e.g. 60% of appointments → 6)
- **Close rate** (e.g. 33% of presentations → 2)
- **Dashboard number** (e.g. 50-10-6-2)

### Rule
The dashboard must stay **consistent week over week** with a gentle gradual increase. Build the business around the rhythm. Do not spike and drop.

## 2. Warm Lead Pipeline (Top-of-Funnel Engine)

Three-stage funnel that produces warm leads who have self-qualified via action.

```
Short-form content → Long-form content → Signal of interest
```

### Stage 1 — Short-form content
- **Format:** 1-3 minute consumable (post, image, short video, carousel)
- **Frequency:** Daily on one platform (small business) / four platforms (scaled)
- **Rule:** People notice you on the **11th impression** within a **90-day window**
- **Content triggers that hook:** Strange/unknown, desirable outcomes, free value, familiar topics
- **Call to action:** Drive to long-form content

### Stage 2 — Long-form content
- **Format:** 10 min to 2 hr (long post, PDF, 10-min video, podcast episode, workshop)
- **Target:** 4 pieces of long-form content built over 12 months
- **Trust threshold:** 2-7 hours of total consumption (Dunbar) before someone feels they know and trust you

### Stage 3 — Signal of interest
- **Action:** Person leaves social media platform, clicks link to landing page, fills a form
- **Signal types:** Waiting list, expression of interest form, assessment, discussion group, live webinar registration
- **This is the lead definition:** Someone who crossed from social media → your property → gave data

### Ramp multipliers
1. Boost highest-performing short-form content with ads
2. Direct messages to carefully selected individuals
3. Joint ventures / sponsorships with non-competitive audiences

## 3. Hook Pitch Template (6-Field Social Pitch)

A paramaterizable 30-45 second pitch. Fill the 6 fields for the current offer.

### Template
```
Name:   <who you are and your business name>
Same:   <put yourself in a category they already understand>
Fame:   <what makes you special — number, award, association>
Pain:   <the frustration they experience before buying from you>
Aim:    <what you aim to do about it>
Game:   <bigger vision of what the business wants to achieve>
```

### Rules
- Fits into 30-45 seconds spoken, or one paragraph in text/email
- Delivered only after reaffirming relevance and (if on phone) asking permission
- Ends with a specific ask: "What I'd love to do is set a time we could talk"

### Three pitch types (escalating depth)
1. **Social pitch** — 30-45s. Name, same, fame, pain, aim, game. Used in social/social media settings
2. **Scheduled pitch** — 15 min to 2 hr. Full tailored presentation. Moves them from path A to path B
3. **Sales pitch** — Transactional. Sell a product or get an investment commitment

## 4. Appointment Booking Sequence (4 Steps)

```
Step 1 — Reaffirm relevance:  "You filled in a form about X..."
Step 2 — Ask permission (phone only): "Have I caught you at a good time? Can I be quick?"
Step 3 — Pitch:  Name/Same/Fame/Pain/Aim/Game (30-45s)
Step 4 — Ask for commitment:  "Here's my diary link / What times work for you?"
```

### Lead qualification (advanced)
When volume scales, add qualifying questions to the signal-of-interest form:
- "Which best describes your current situation?"
- "Which outcome are you looking for?"
- "Which types of solutions do you have in mind?"

Use these to segment who gets an appointment vs nurture.

## 5. Sales Presentation — 10-Component Structure

A complete sales call follows this sequence. Do not skip, do not reorder.

### Phase 1 — Set the stage (components 1-3)
1. **Framing** — Everything the prospect experiences before and at the start. Location, virtual background, advance communication. Frame communicates trust and authority before you speak.
2. **Rapport** — 2-5 mins. Use their name, find common interest, smile, be polite, be complimentary, reference mutual connections.
3. **Permission to sell** — "We can chitchat or I have a process where I ask questions and give recommendations. Which would you prefer?"

### Phase 2 — Listen and understand (components 4-6)
*Goal: they talk, you listen. Fill three baskets:*

4. **Present situation** — Current state. What's less than perfect about it. Questions about their reality.
5. **Prize (desired outcome)** — The movie they play in their head of what owning your solution would be like. People buy access to the prize, not the product.
6. **Problem** — Obstacles, criteria, past attempts that blocked the prize.

### Phase 3 — Speak and teach (components 7-9)
*Switch from listening to speaking. Must go in order:*

7. **Insight** — A framework or research finding that gives them a new perspective on their situation. NOT about your product. Establishes authority. (e.g. "The entrepreneurial journey" or "The role of hormones in weight loss")
8. **Method** — How to solve it. The step-by-step approach. (e.g. "The 5 Ps to becoming a key person of influence")
9. **Solution** — Your product/service presented with visual aids (brochure, slide deck, physical model). 3 tiers: bronze / silver / gold.

### Phase 4 — Discuss and complete (component 10)
10. **Discussion → Completion**
    - "How does that land for you?"
    - Silence. Let them think (up to 15-20 seconds).
    - Handle objections using the Ball Boy Protocol (see below).
    - Complete: onboard, paperwork, CRM update, or schedule next appointment.

## 6. Ball Boy Protocol (Objection Handling)

**Never play conversational tennis** (question → answer → question → answer, back and forth).

### The protocol:
```
Prospect:  "I'm not sure about the price."
You:       "OK, price is important. Is there anything else?"  [catch the ball, hold it]

Prospect:  "And the timing doesn't feel right."
You:       "Timing matters too. Let me note that. Anything else?"  [catch, hold]

Prospect:  "That's it."
You:       [Now respond to ALL objections grouped together]
```

### Rules
- Do not answer until all objections are surfaced
- Write them down visibly (real or virtual notepad)
- Respond to the whole set at once

## 7. Golf Bag of Insights & Methods

Maintain 5-10 pre-built insight→method pairs, each tagged by situation type.

### Each entry:
```
Situation:  <what the prospect says / what their prize or problem is>
Insight:    <the framework or research that reframes their situation>
Method:     <the step-by-step approach that solves it>
```

### How it works
- Prospect describes their situation → you select the right "club" from the bag
- You've rehearsed and practiced each one
- You don't create on the fly — you deploy pre-built material

### Sample entries by situation:
| Prospect says... | Pull insight | Then method |
|---|---|---|
| "I can't grow past where I am" | Entrepreneurial journey / founder-led growth | 5 Ps framework |
| "I've tried diets and nothing works" | Role of hormones in weight loss | Sleep / exercise / diet / supplementation |
| "I don't know how to position myself" | Authority gap / 11th impression rule | Content engine methodology |

## 8. Follow-Up Sequence (7-Touch + Nurture + Reactivation)

### Peak window: 48 hours after presentation
This is when commitments happen. Follow up fast — within minutes, not hours.

### 7-touch follow-up plan
Reasons to get back in touch (not "just checking in"):
1. Special offer / deadline
2. New insight or research to share
3. New customer testimonial video
4. Follow-up on something discussed (the holiday, the board approval)
5. New data or research just published
6. Commentary on industry news
7. "Are you still working on X?"

### Nurture sequence (post-7-touch)
Friendly, low-pressure touch points via social media or email:
- Testimonial videos
- New insights
- Special offers
- News commentary
- "Are you still interested in [outcome]?"
- New research/data

### Reactivation campaign (when nurture stalls)
Send: **"Have you given up on [outcome]?"**
- "We haven't heard from you. Is this still important? Have you solved it? Have you given up?"
- If still interested → send an assessment or calculator to re-engage
- If they re-engage → they go back to the top of the pipeline as a lead

## 11. Problem → Process → Prize Framework (Marketing Playground)

Replace the old "marketing funnel" (pressure-based) with a "marketing playground" (experience-based). People do not like to be funneled.

### The three zones customers move through:

```
Zone 1: Problem aware   →   Zone 2: Process aware   →   Zone 3: Prize aware
```

### Zone 1 — Problem (empathy and diagnosis)
Customers know something is wrong but don't know how to solve it. Content must have **radical empathy** — show you understand their frustration.

**Clinical method for problem-aware content:**
```
Symptoms  →  Causes  →  Treatments  →  Prognosis
```
- Symptoms: What's going wrong that they notice day-to-day
- Causes: Why it's happening
- Treatments: What to do about it
- Prognosis: How long to fix it

**Assets for Zone 1:**
- Mini-course on the problem
- Problem diagnosis quiz
- Discussion group (WhatsApp, Facebook) for people struggling with the problem

### Zone 2 — Process (best practices and methodology)
Customers want to discover what works. They want to understand the approach.

**Assets for Zone 2:**
- Book about the process
- Podcast episodes explaining the methodology
- Case studies showing what happened when someone went through the process
- Live workshop introducing best practices
- Assessment that benchmarks where they're strong/weak

### Zone 3 — Prize (solution and payoff)
Customers want to confirm this solution will give them the outcome.

**Assets for Zone 3:**
- 1-to-1 sales meeting
- Free trial
- Experience event with existing customers
- ROI calculator
- Explainer video / unboxing

### How an agent uses this
**ContentStrategist:** Map every piece of content to one of the three zones. Don't skip ahead. Build zone-1 content first (problem awareness), then zone-2 (process), then zone-3 (prize).

**EmailMarketingExpert:** Sequence emails in this order — first empathize with the problem, then teach the process, then present the prize.

## 12. Product Market Fit (PMF) Process

PMF is when your ideal customer persona meets your intellectual property packaged as an offer. Customer would be disappointed if you went out of business.

### Step 1 — Surveys to get inside the ICP's mind

**Survey A: Waiting list campaign (pre-launch)**
Launch a landing page saying "new approach to [outcome] — join the waiting list." Ask 5 questions:
1. Which best describes your current situation? (list options)
2. Which best describes the desired outcome in the next 90 days? (list options)
3. Which best describes the frustration/obstacle in your way? (list options)
4. Which best describes your budget or preferred solution type? (list options)
5. Is there anything else you want us to know? (open box)

**Survey B: PMF survey (existing customers)**
Single most important question: "How would you feel if you no longer had access to [product]?"
- **Very disappointed** → Your ICP. Follow up: who are they, what do they rely on most?
- **Somewhat disappointed** → Needs improvement. Ask: what features/services would tip them over?
- **Would be okay** → Ignore. Not your ICP.

**Survey C: Experience score (service businesses)**
- "How would you rate your experience with us today?"
- Followed by: situation, outcome, frustration, "What could we do to improve by at least 1 point?"

### Step 2 — Gold / Silver / Bronze offer construction
Build three versions of your offer:
- **Bronze** — 4-5 key things needed for the outcome. Healthy margin, lower price.
- **Silver** — Basics + a few frills. Middle price point.
- **Gold** — Everything you can think of. 5-10 elements. Highest price, highest margin.

All three profitable. Present with visual aids (brochure/slide deck). 50% of the brain is visual — if they can't see it, they can't buy it.

### Step 3 — 30 sales meetings validation
Present the new offer to 30 people one-to-one. Target: at least 3 commit to Silver or Gold.

### Step 4 — 90-day test
After 90 days, ask: "Would you be disappointed if we took this away?"
Pass = they say yes, rate experience 9-10, would enthusiastically recommend.

## 13. 5 A's (Goal Setting & Execution System)

A quarterly operating system that turns vision into weekly execution. Audit every 90 days.

```
Alignment → Awareness → Accountabilities → Activities → Assets
```

### Alignment
Feeling of being pulled into the future. Three choices:
- **Lifestyle business** — fun, freedom, flexibility. 4-12 people, smaller
- **Performance business** — built to sell. 30+ people, executive team, stress
- **Hybrid** — performance business that funds lifestyle

Key actions:
- Define end state (3 years from now, see #18 for full journey context)
- Study people who have that end state
- Understand the detailed roadmap
- Align those around you (spouse, co-founder, team)
- Realign every 90 days (assumption: alignment only lasts 90 days)

### Awareness
Surface everything that could block the goal. Keep an **awareness list** (rolling document, accessible to all).
- "Can I raise an awareness with you?" — teach the language
- Company-wide: leadership team awareness list, broader team awareness list
- When awarenesses aren't raised, the team is pretending. The same problems haunt you forever.

### Accountabilities
The big things that must be solved in the next 90 days. Who is responsible.
- Use the "Who, Not How" principle — assemble people who can solve each problem
- 2-4 major breakthroughs per quarter

### Activities
The weekly repetition that drives results. Same stuff over and over.

**Weekly rhythm:**
- **Monday morning meeting** — everyone declares 3-6 things they'll accomplish this week. Team suggests additions/subtractions
- **Friday afternoon debrief** — check off: done / not done. Brief, no big explanations, just accountability

**Quarterly reset:** 3-6 big things to accomplish in the next 90 days that move the needle. Half-day to full-day offsite.

### Assets
Three options for every investment: **Buy / Develop / Improve**
- Buy the asset (existing)
- Develop from scratch (innovate)
- Improve what you have (refresh)

Types of assets to consider: YouTube channel, lead generation assets, book, website, property portfolio, stocks, pension. Prioritize by ROI (a $10K lead gen asset can outperform a $1M property).

## 14. Product Ecosystem (4 Products)

Four connected products that form one smooth customer journey. Each does a different job.

```
Gift → Product for Prospects → Core Offering → Product for Clients
```

### Gift (free)
- Something free with no risk — free trial, free account, free download
- Fully digital, scales infinitely
- Feels valuable, gives an immediate quick win
- **Job:** Gets attention

### Product for Prospects (low commitment)
- Small time or money commitment to get started
- Formats: 90-min workshop, 30-day trial at low price, book, online assessment
- **Job:** Builds trust
- The assessment is the most powerful form: 15 questions, qualifies the lead, segments them

### Core Offering (transformation)
- The main thing you're known for
- Formats: consulting package, agency services, training program, software subscription + setup fee
- Presented with **visual aids** — brochure, slide deck, physical model
- 3 tiers: bronze/silver/gold
- **Job:** Delivers the transformation

### Product for Clients (retention)
- Subscription, membership, community, annual retreat
- Keeps customers connected after the core experience
- **Job:** Maintains relationship over time

**Profit principle:** The profit is not in any single product. It's in the ecosystem flow. Marketing drives people Gift → Prospect → Core. Delivery drives Core → Client.

## 15. Three Growth Campaigns (Annual Rhythm)

Run these three campaign types every year.

### Campaign 1: Perfect Repeatable Week (weekly)
The baseline. Same lead generation + sales activities every single week.
- Generate leads → book appointments → present → close
- Examples: run a 90-min intro workshop every Wednesday, drive people to an online assessment every week, drive people to free account signups every week
- **Job:** Consistent baseline revenue

### Campaign 2: Quarterly Spotlight Campaign (quarterly)
Something special, different, or seasonal.
- Special event, product launch, Black Friday, Easter special, summer catalog, sponsorship
- **Job:** Profit spikes (icing on the cake)

### Campaign 3: Annual Big Message (yearly)
The big ideas you want to share for the year.
- Generate lots of content (short-form + long-form) around 1-3 core big ideas
- Share across all platforms
- Example: this YouTube channel is an annual big message
- **Job:** Build the brand, grow followers and fans over time

## 16. Selling to High-Ticket (Rich People)

Top 10% of people have 60% of the spending capital. Three principles.

### Principle 1: Powerful pitch
Use the Hook Pitch (#3). Rich people meet 100 people/month — they need a tight, rehearsed pitch. Always ask permission before pitching.

### Principle 2: Contextual adjacency
People judge you by what surrounds you. Before you open your mouth, your context has already spoken.

**Six contextual markers:**
1. **Books/thought leaders/courses** — what you're currently studying, who you follow
2. **Educational institutions** — where you studied, credentials
3. **Locations you meet in** — Members clubs, beautiful hotels, premium cafes (not Starbucks). Every city has rich-people locations
4. **Brands surrounding you** — Crisp, premium. Apple vs cracked Android. Ralph Lauren vs band t-shirt. Dubai vs London norms differ
5. **Suppliers you share** — Rich people's banks, accountants, lawyers. They run monthly events — get on the list
6. **Mutual contacts** — Check LinkedIn/Instagram for mutual connections before meeting

**Rule:** You need 3-5 of these markers working for you before you speak. Entry-level entrepreneurs never think about context. Rich people put thought into it.

### Principle 3: Land & Expand
Prove yourself small → get invited to do big.

```
Proof (free) → Review (documented) → Small deal → Review (documented) → "I'm getting busy" → Big deal
```

**Proof phase:** Education (share insights they didn't know) or Research (collect data they didn't have) or a short free sprint (e.g. "let's just go for a run together" for a fitness trainer).

**Review process:** Always write it down and print it. Printed document. Rich people don't have head space. They need something visual to share with their executive assistant, financial adviser, spouse. 50% of brain is visual.

**Propose a deal:** Ask "What do you think the deal should look like?" Rich people are great at constructing deals. They often suggest something bigger than you would.

**Constraint naming:** After delivering 2 successful projects, say: "I'm getting busy. Do you think I should double down working with you, or part ways?" Most say "let's do bigger stuff."

**Referrals:** After big deal closes, ask: "Do you know of any big deals in your network I should be pitching for?"

## 17. Online Assessment Lead Generation Machine

A complete lead generation system built around a 15-question quiz on a landing page.

### Landing page (4 elements)
1. **Hook** — One of two types:
   - *Frustration hook:* "Frustrated that X happens even though you do Y?"
   - *Readiness hook:* "Are you ready to [desired outcome]?"
2. **Value proposition** — "Answer 15 questions to measure and improve 3 key areas" (list the 3 areas)
3. **Credibility** — Who created it (bio), what research/statistics back it up ("85% of people struggle with X," "Harvard study found...")
4. **Call to action** — "Start the quiz. 3 minutes. Free. Immediate recommendations."

Target conversion: 20-40% of page visitors start the quiz.

### 15 questions (3 parts)

**Part 1 — Contact info (mandatory):** Name, email. Location from IP (auto). Phone (optional).

**Part 2 — 10 best-practice questions:**
Ask if they're doing 10 things they should be doing. Yes/no or scaled. This generates their score. (e.g. "Do you track calories? Do you work out 3x/week?")

**Part 3 — 5 qualification questions (for YOU, the seller):**
1. Which best describes your current situation? (4-5 options)
2. Which outcome do you want in the next 90 days? (list options)
3. What obstacle is stopping you / what have you tried that didn't work? (list options)
4. Which solution suits you best? (education, coaching, software, done-for-you — implies budget level)
5. Is there anything else we should know? (open box — people reveal purchase intent, deadlines, and budgets here)

### Dynamic results page
- Big reveal: score, speedometer, thermometer (cold/warm/hot)
- 3 insights based on their answers
- Next steps vary by lead quality:
  - **Hot/qualified:** Offer 1-to-1 meeting
  - **Warm/middle:** Offer group presentation or event
  - **Cold/wrong fit:** Offer content (video, podcast, book recommendation)

## 18. Entrepreneurial Journey (Full 6-Step Framework)

The complete path from idea to exit. Every agent should understand where a business is in this journey to write appropriate content.

```
FO fit → MVP test → PMF → Go to market → Scale up → Exit
```

### Step 1 — Founder Opportunity Fit
Soul-searching: origin story + mission + vision + where you're close to money.
**Formula:** Passion × Payment × Pain = Founder Opportunity Fit
- Passion: what you care about / your mission
- Payment: proximity to people with budgets
- Pain: a market with a big problem

### Step 2 — Viability Testing (MVP)
Fast, cheap experiments. Best method: **Waiting list campaign.**
- Create a landing page with a hook
- Promote it to see how many join
- Ask qualification questions (situation, outcome, obstacle, budget)
- Compare multiple ideas (one got 720, another got 4,500 — clear winner)

### Step 3 — Product Market Fit
30-150 real paying customers. Talk to every one of them. Find the subgroup who'd be "very disappointed" if you disappeared. Requires a team of 4 (leader, sales, product, ops).

### Step 4 — Go to Market
LAPS rhythm. One route to market, one customer type, one presentation, one ad, one landing page. Focused execution. Team of 6-12 people, typically 8. Target: $1M+ revenue.

### Step 5 — Scale Up (optional but high-reward)
Launch new products into new markets in new territories. Jump from <12 to 30+ people. 5-person leadership team (CEO, CFO, CTO, COO, CMO). 4 teams × ~10 people: Growth, Product, Data/Insights, Senior Leadership.

**Crossing the desert (13-29 people):** Too big to be small, too small to be big. Profits disappear into management overhead. Most businesses die here.

**Scale-up actions:**
- Formalize assets: brand book, culture book, IP registration, explainer videos, training videos
- Improve quality of earnings: prioritize recurring revenue over one-off, scalable product over unscalable service
- Understand EBITDA, unit economics

### Step 6 — Exit
Non-negotiable. You will exit — by sale, by death, or by moving on.

**Who buys:**
- **Strategic buyers** — fits with what they already have (pay highest)
- **Financial buyers** — fund via debt, business services the debt
- **Trophy buyers** — love the awards, team, product (emotional)

**Exit mechanics:**
- Beautiful 5-year financial forecast showing what happens if they buy
- Documented asset catalog (YouTube channel with 1000 videos included in purchase)
- Multiple buyers competing = bidding war (120M → 220M in 24 hours with 2 buyers)

### Which path?
- **Lifestyle business (4-12 people):** Fun, freedom, flexible. Difficult to sell (founder-dependent). Usually a 3-year earnout. Most keep it.
- **Performance business (30+):** Built to sell. Stress but bigger payday. Requires geeking out on business itself (dashboards, offsites, management theory) plus your domain.
- **Struggling boutique:** Same size as lifestyle but doesn't make money. Commoditized, no brand, no digital. Just paying wages.

## 19. Lifestyle Business Blueprint (4-12 Person Team)

### The 8 key roles
1. **Key Person of Influence (you/founder)** — your job is the 5 P's: Pitch, Publish, Product, Profile, Partnerships. NOT running the business.
2. **General Manager** — runs the business. A 24-year-old who managed a cafe can run your business.
3. **Head of Marketing** — demand generation
4. **Head of Sales** — closing
5. **Finance** — money management
6. **IT** — data and systems
7. **Product Development & Delivery** — builds and delivers
8. **Customer Success** — keeps customers happy

**Founder's 5 P's:** Pitching the business, Publishing content, Creating new products, Raising your Profile, Strategic Partnerships.
**What you STOP doing:** Running the business. Replace yourself with a GM.

### Team operating rhythm
- **One communication channel** (WhatsApp group for small teams, Slack for larger)
- **Tech stack:** CRM, AI provider, office tools, lead gen tools (ScoreApp)
- **File storage system:** Everyone knows where things go
- **Sleep-at-night dashboard:** Cash at bank, database size, red flags/issues, upcoming milestones

### The $125K/person floor
Each person on the core team needs ~$125K USD minimum revenue to stay profitable.
- 4 people → $500K revenue floor
- 12 people → $1.5M revenue floor

## 20. Key Person of Influence (5 P's Framework)

The transition from "worker" to "key person of influence" (KPI).

### The power law
In every industry: vast majority earn $50-150K (workers). Top 5% earn exponentially more (KPIs). Newbies are learning. Workers have skills but not fame. KPIs are the most connected, well-known, and highest-paid.

### The merry-go-round of distraction
Worker in industry A sees KPI in industry B → switches to industry B → becomes worker in industry B → repeats. Chasing shiny objects. Never becomes KPI in any industry.

**Fix:** Choose one industry you love. Become the KPI in that industry.

### The 5 P's

**P1 — Pitching:** The journey of a thousand pitches. Pitch ideas, not products. Three types: social (30s), scheduled (15min-2hr), sales (transactional). You get what you pitch for — intentionally or not.

**P2 — Publishing:** Put your ideas in the public domain. Video, podcast, book. Author = authority. **Prolific beats perfect** — be a directionalist, not a perfectionist. The Beatles wrote 400 songs, 30-40 were hits.

**P3 — Product:** Don't sell time for money. Create a product ecosystem (#14). Income follows assets, and product IP is the best asset.

**P4 — Profile (SALT framework):**
- **S**ocial media accounts — at least 4 platforms, regular presence
- **A**wards and associations — win awards, associate with known brands
- **L**ive events — speak at, host, sponsor
- **T**hird-party platforms — other people's podcasts, traditional media

**Principle:** You are who Google says you are. Every opportunity stands behind a Google search.

**Profile mindset:** Don't chase the spotlight. Become the spotlight. Not "look at me" — "look at this." Get famous through client success, not self-promotion.

**P5 — Partnerships:** Only KPIs can do partnerships. One + one = 11.
- **Brand partnerships** — partner with someone more known
- **Product partnerships** — bundle your product with another (Porsche + Bose)
- **Distribution partnerships** — get your product in front of their audience (Walmart, Virgin)

## 21. Confidence = Data (30/150 Test)

Confidence is the ability to predict what will happen next. Prediction comes from data points.

### The confidence framework
| Data points | Confidence level | What you know |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | Beginner's luck / newbie confidence | Nothing. Optimistic curiosity |
| 30 | ~65% confidence | First confidence interval. You can roughly predict |
| 150 | ~95% confidence | Strong confidence. You can reliably predict |

### How to apply
- **Sales meetings:** 30 meetings → 65% confident in close rate. 150 meetings → 95% confident
- **Content:** 30 posts → 65% confident in engagement. 150 posts → 95% confident
- **Ads:** $X to get 30 clicks → 65% confident in CPA. More $ to get 150 clicks → 95% confident
- **Public speaking:** 30 talks → 65% confident. 150 talks → 95% confident
- **Hiring:** 30 candidate interviews → 65% confident in hire quality
- **Product:** 30 paying customers → 65% confident in PMF. 150 → 95%

### The mindset shift
Don't focus on the feeling of lacking confidence. The feeling only tells you: **"I don't have enough data yet."** Suspend your feelings until after you've collected 30-150 samples.

The goal: Become a **passionate visionary scientist** — passionate because you care, visionary because you have an opinion of the future, scientist because you collect data and calculate confidence.

### Speed principle
Confidence = speed. People who move fast (Steven Bartlett doing a full day: gym, keynote, online event, pitch deck, airport, product planning) have confidence from massive data sets.

## 22. Monetize Your IP (Proof Story → Product → Scale)

### Step 1 — Find your proof story
Criteria: A time in the last 5 years where you:
1. Did something special
2. That interests an ideal customer persona (ICP)
3. Achieved a quantifiable outcome
4. Gave that outcome a name
5. Can explain how you did it step by step

This is your **intellectual property** — your unique story with your emotions, your steps, your take on things.

### Step 2 — Create two products
- **Product for prospects (learn):** Webinar, online assessment, mini-course. Landing page that teaches the IP.
- **Core offering (deliver):** Agency, software, consulting, physical product. Slide deck/brochure that shows what gets delivered and the price.

### Step 3 — Attention (NKR framework)
```
Noticed → Known → Rated
```
- **Noticed:** 11 short-form pieces within 90 days. Post daily.
- **Known:** 2-7 hours of long-form content (podcast guesting, live workshops, keynote speeches, book)
- **Rated:** 4 associated brands establish your price tier (discount/mass market/niche/luxury). You get rated through association.

### Step 4 — Signals of interest
Landing page with Hook + Value Prop + Credibility + CTA. See #17 for full structure.

### Step 5 — Sales conversions (LAPS)
Track: Leads → Appointments → Presentations → Sales. Use a dashboard. Improve each conversion rate week over week.

### Step 6 — Scale with math
**Calculate allowable cost per sale:**
- Core offering price: $5,000
- % happy to spend on acquisition: 15%
- Allowable cost per sale: $750

**Calculate allowable cost per lead:**
- If 3 out of 100 leads convert: $750 / (100/3) = $22.50 per lead

**Scale up spending:**
- Ads (Google, Facebook, LinkedIn, X)
- Joint ventures (pay influencers/orgs to promote)
- Paid outreach (pay someone to call or DM)
- Sponsorships (newsletter, YouTube channel, event)

Ratchet up monthly: $10K → $12K → $15K → $20K. Always check against allowable cost per lead/sale.

## 23. Mindset: Creator vs Consumer

The fundamental choice in an AI-disrupted world.

| Employee mindset | Entrepreneur mindset |
|---|---|
| My value = execute best practice | Inefficiency = opportunity |
| Deviation is risk | Optimization is the game |
| AI makes me suboptimal | AI makes everything sub-optimal → everything can be improved |
| Hyper-consume (YouTube, TikTok, retraining) | Create, improve, disrupt, transform |
| "Show up and do a good job" | "The world is my playground" |

**The whole schooling system prepares you for the employee mindset. The entrepreneurial mindset is foreign and must be chosen.**
