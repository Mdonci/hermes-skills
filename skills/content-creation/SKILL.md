# Content Creation Skills

## Overview

Content creation skills based on the 4321 Content Flywheel and SLAY Framework. These skills enable high-velocity content production that bypasses the "stealth research" phase where 85% of buyers make decisions before meeting you.

---

## Core Frameworks

### 4321 Content Flywheel

**Description:** Content operating system for consistent momentum and authority building.

**Origin:** Lara Acosta

**Components:**
- **4 posts per week:** Consistent momentum to bypass stealth research phase
- **3 content types:**
  - **Story (Relatability):** Journey from Sybrillo founder to Fractional CMO architect
  - **Educate (80% of content):** Modular 'How-I' lessons from Insight Builder and MAC
  - **Sell (20% of content):** Direct evidence of $4M ARR scales and Globee-winning turnarounds
- **2 target personas:**
  - **Founder:** Economic Buyer obsessed with gross margins
  - **Marketing Director:** Implementer drowning in attribution complexity
- **1 lead magnet:** Rotating asset to move 'rented' attention to 'owned' list

**Content Mix Rule:** 80% Educate, 20% Sell — maintain ratio for trust building

---

### SLAY Framework

**Description:** Visual rhythm for mobile-first content (60-70% mobile dwell time). Maximizes engagement on mobile devices.

**Origin:** Lara Acosta

**Components:**
- **S_story:** Start with 8-10 word hook
  - Example: "I asked 100 founders why their ads were failing."
- **L_lesson:** One sharp insight
  - Example: "Ad creative is a symptom; broken buying logic is the disease."
- **A_actionable:** Give them a tool
  - Example: "Use the Transformation Map to find your Snap Point before you open Meta Ads."
- **Y_you_engagement:** Ask a polarizing question
  - Example: "Is your brand narrative easy for a customer to repeat? Tell me below."

**Tactical Patterns:**
- hook-first writing
- modular insight delivery
- tool-based CTAs
- engagement prompting

---

### Outlier Content Method

**Description:** Turning one human truth into four content angles.

**Week Referenced:** Week 12

**Principle:** One core insight, multiple angle variations for different platforms and audience segments.

### Story First Selling

**Description:** People buy stories first, evidence second.

**Principle:** Lead with narrative, support with proof. The story creates emotional buy-in before logical evidence.

**Tactical Patterns:**
- hook-first writing
- modular insight delivery
- tool-based CTAs

## Welcome Subscriber Flow Pipeline

**Two-phase pipeline for email welcome flows. Not one-shot delegation.**

David requires this exact structure for welcome subscriber email work:

### Phase 1 — ContentStrategist defines the strategy
Before any copy is written:
- Determine the **entry intent** (what actually brought them here?)
- Map the **belief journey** (before -> after per email)
- Analyze the **key tension** (the central emotional/psychological friction)
- Build the **AIM map** (Acknowledge -> Include -> Mobilize per next_gen_welcome_flow_sop.md)
- Define trust architecture, claims to avoid, rhetorical balance
- Output: Strategy brief with copy briefs for each email

### Phase 2 — Copywriter (Claude Sonnet 4.6) writes the copy
Based on the strategy brief:
- Follow the AIM framework per next_gen_welcome_flow_sop.md
- MUST call skill_view(name='writing-quality') and apply full audit before delivering
- Each email must show: AIM phase, persuasion balance, belief shift, open loop closure
- No em dashes, no AI-isms, no generic "welcome to the family" language

### Entry-Intent Adaptation
The entry point changes the entire flow. **Do not assume "welcome subscriber = generic welcome."**

| Entry Type | Key Tension | Trust Architecture Priority |
|------------|-------------|---------------------------|
| Indiegogo/Kickstarter waitlist | "I want this but crowdfunding has burned me before" | Prototype evidence, team legitimacy, campaign mechanics, manufacturing readiness, honest risk disclosure |
| Direct purchase waitlist | "Is it worth the price? Will it actually ship?" | Price anchoring, delivery timeline, return policy |
| Newsletter / content subscriber | "Why should I care about another brand?" | Story, worldview, mission, values |
| Lead magnet download | "Give me the thing I signed up for" | Deliver promised asset, then build relationship |
| Product launch / pre-order | "Is this real? What do I get?" | Product readiness, specifications, launch timeline |

### Indiegogo/Crowdfunding Welcome Flow — Specific Rules
- **Name the tension directly:** Subscribers carry unresolved anxiety about crowdfunding failure. Acknowledge it honestly. Do not pretend it does not exist.
- **Must address:** Team credibility (where, who, track record), prototype/production evidence (working unit, tested in field), manufacturing location and method, campaign mechanics (Indiegogo protection, not guarantees), shipping timeline honesty, honest "why crowdfunding" explanation.
- **Claims to avoid:** No shipping date that is not firm, no "guaranteed delivery" beyond Indiegogo's actual terms, no "risk-free" (crowdfunding has inherent risk), no "prices will never be this low again" unless documented policy.
- **Dos:** "We have built it. We have tested it. The campaign funds production, not research." Honest risk acknowledgment paired with concrete de-risking evidence.
- **Belief journey target:** "I know exactly what tier I am backing and why. The risk feels acceptable. I am ready for launch day."
